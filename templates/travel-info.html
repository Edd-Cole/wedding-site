Travel

Depending on your country of departure, there will be several ways to travel to Aurangabad. The main focus will be flying as any other method takes a while

Albeit, the routes will look very similar. There are 2 main routes to travel to Aurangabad.

  1. Country of Departure --> New Delhi --> Aurangabad
  2. Country of Departure --> Mumbai --> Aurangabad

United Kingdom

The first leg of your journey will be travelling into either Mumbai or New Delhi.

The main airline providers that you will want to look at for your journey into India will be:

  • Air India,
  • British Airways,
  • Virgin Atlantic,
  • Lufthansa,
  • Air Swiss,
  • Many more, you can check them out here

Then, you will need a connecting flight into Aurangabad, this will most likely be provided by Air India.

Return flights for 1 person will be in the range of £600 - £800 depending on when you travel.

Here are a few tips you can use to save yourself some money

EU

Much like the UK, the first leg of your journey will be travelling into either New Delhi or Mumbai.

However, depending on where you live within the EU, you may need to get an additional connecting flight to get to an airport that facilitates travel to India.

Here are a list of airports you can use if you need an additional connecting flight:

  • London Heathrow,
  • Rome Flumicino,
  • Paris Charles de Gaulle,
  • Frankfurt am Main,
  • Munich International,
  • Istanbul International

When you are at one of these airports, or one that let's you travel directly to India, here are some airlines you can use:

  • ITA Airways,
  • British Airways,
  • Air India,
  • Lufthansa,

After you have landed in India, you will need to get a final connecting flight into Aurangabad.

Return flights for 1 person will be in the range of €600 - €800 depending on when you travel and from where you start.

Here are a few tips you can use to save yourself some money

Egypt

If you are flying to India from Egypt, you will need to get a connecting flight to India. Fortunately, as you are closer to India, you won't go too far out of your way in your travels and will have cheaper prices.

The main destinations you will connect to before entering India will be:

  • Bahrain, Bahrain,
  • Riyadh, Saudi Arabia,
  • jeddah, Saudi Arabia,
  • Kuwait, Kuwait

After this you will travel straight to you preferred port of Delhi or Mumbai.

Your choice of airlines are:

  • Gulf Air,
  • Flynas,
  • Saudia,
  • Qatar Airways

Then you will get a connecting flight to Aurangabad, most likely provided by Air India.

Return flights for 1 person will be in the range of 6,000 LE - 8,500 LE.
