Visa Applications

You will need a visa to enter India. This is a VERY important step. don't miss this out!

What Visa Do You Need?

You can find the general process and information for a visa application here.

  • For UK & Egyptian Citizens, follow the instructions below for paper applications
  • For American, Danish, and Spanish Citizens, follow the eVisa instructions here
  • For any other nationality, you can find out whether you need an eVisa or paper visa here

Getting a Paper Application Form

To get a paper application form, you will need to fill out the following online form here.

In order to fill it out completely, you will need the following items:

  • Your Passport
  • A digital 2 x 2 inch passport style photo. It will need to be between 10 - 300 KB, and must be a JPEG document type. Refer below for an example image
  • If you have previously been to India, you will need your old Visa

Important Tips

  • Your visa starts at the date of issue, not when you first land, be sure that you make the visa at least 6 months long!
  • If you make a mistake after completing the form, you will have to create another form. You cannot edit it out
  • If you want to change your application form after you have booked your appointment, it is fine to do so. I made 3 submissions and only ever had 1 appointment they do not check the online form at the Indian Mission, only the paper copies
  • If you need an appointment now but don't have all the docs you need, use false data, get the appointment, and be sure to come back and resubmit a new form
  • I highly recommend getting the photos done professionally, I saw a few people have to redo their entire application because they did not have the correct photo uploaded or with them at the Indian Mission
  • Do as much of the application, printing and preparation for your visa at home. The Indian Mission does have a photocopier and 2 computers, but you will have to pay hefty prices to use them

Booking your Appointment

There are a couple of things you will need in order to book your appointment:

  • A completed application form, you can fill it out here.
  • A date and time that you can make, the earliest appointment that is available is typically one month away
  • If you are planning on going to an Indian Mission as a group, each person will need an appointment.

When you have your completed form, you can book your appointment here.

Attending Your Visa Appointment

You MUST bring to the Indian Mission:

  • A printed out completed visa application form, fill out here
  • A printed out copy of your appointment date and time, fill out here
  • Your passport with at least 6 months before expiration & 2 free blank pages
  • A printed out page of your passport photo page and adjacent page, make sure it is signed if there is a place to sign
  • 2, 2 x 2 inch square photos, guidelines can be found here
  • A declaration form, printed and signed, can be found here. If you are going into the Indian Mission they also give them out

    • Additionally, depending on your circumstances, you may need the following completed:

  • If you have previously been to India you must provide a photocopy of your previous Indian Visa
  • If you intend for someone else to attend the Indian Mission in your place you must sign an authorisation form, can be found here

Covid Advice

The British Government has stated that travel to India is currently safe.

Vaccinated

If you are fully vaccinated, you will need to do the following:

Pre-Boarding

  • Submit a self declaration form, here

On Arrival

  • Self monitor for the next 14 days from your arrival into India
  • You may randomly be selected to have your temperature taken in the port of your arrival
  • You may be asked to present your self declaration form, vaccination certificate or negative PCR test to staff

Additional guidelines are available here.

Un-vaccinated

If you are not fully vaccinated, you will need to do the following:

Pre-Boarding

  • You will need to upload a negative PRC test that has been taken 72 hours before travel
  • You will need to submit a declaration confirming the legitimacy of the test, failure to do so will result in criminal prosecution
  • Your test must meet the Indian guidelines and cannot be provided by the NHS

On Arrival

  • Self monitor for the next 14 days from your arrival into India
  • You may randomly be selected to have your temperature taken in the port of your arrival
  • You may be asked to present your self declaration form, vaccination certificate or negative PCR test to staff

Additional guidelines are available here.
